Agriculture

AB Bureau legally owns 20 square miles of a registered agricultural land, ready for private investment for massive crop production. It has to be noted that in South Sudan, more than ninety percent (90%) of its land is arable, with the potential to become the breadbasket of Africa and the Middle East inclusive. But only four percent (4%) of the land is cultivated today, and worse more South Sudan continues to rely on agricultural imports from Uganda and Kenya and occasionally from the Sudan and Tanzania. Cereals, primarily sorghum and maize, millet, sesame and rice are the dominant staple crops in South Sudan (The 2009 National. Baseline Household Survey). Mining, Energy & Cement Plants

These are big business opportunities in the Republic of South Sudan where the private sector investors could reliably invest in them. It is to be noted therein hat; mining sector particularly gold mining remains undeveloped. Cement plants and steel metal factories are none-existent in the young country placing high construction costs in the country. Therefore, any investment in the cement factory, steel metal factories, and related powerplants would generate high returns in very short time largely because its scarce in the construction sector. It has to be noted herein that EAB Bureau has identified gold areas and locations where private capital maybe interested to invest therein. On the other hand, energy and electricity particularly sola system constitute important source of power particularly in the rural South Sudan which lacks power supplies.
